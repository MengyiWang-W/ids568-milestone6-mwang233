Definition:
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is an architecture that enhances large language models by retrieving relevant external documents at inference time instead of relying solely on pre-trained knowledge.

Key Components:
- Document chunking: splitting documents into smaller segments
- Embedding model: converting text into vector representations
- Vector database: storing embeddings for efficient similarity search
- Retriever: selecting top-k relevant chunks
- Generator: producing answers based on retrieved context

Advantages:
- Access to up-to-date information
- Ability to incorporate proprietary or domain-specific data
- Reduced hallucination compared to pure LLM generation

Limitations:
- Strong dependence on retrieval quality
- Errors in retrieval propagate to generation
- Context window limits may restrict usable information

Example Scenario:
If a user asks about a specific regulation not present in the training data, RAG can retrieve the relevant document and generate a grounded response.

Failure Case:
If the retriever fails to find the relevant chunk, the model may generate incomplete or incorrect answers even when the knowledge exists in the corpus.
